01 · Problem

The gap between a logged meal plan and an Instacart cart.

Every day, millions of people open MyFitnessPal and explicitly declare what they intend to eat. They set macro targets, log meals, and identify deficits. This is purchase intent data: more precise and more honest than anything a search algorithm can infer. Instacart's ad platform reaches users only after they open the app. MFP users are upstream. That moment is unaddressed.

No calorie tracker has a checkout integration today. Not because the demand is not there, but because no grocery platform has owned the partnership layer. MFP is owned by Francisco Partners, a PE firm with independent commercial interests. The deal requires revenue-sharing and data governance that only a scaled commerce player like Instacart has the CPG relationships to structure. The whitespace is a distribution problem, not a demand problem.

Dimension
Current State and Gap

Target User

MFP users with 30+ day logging streaks who order groceries at least twice monthly. Sufficient data density for intent inference, demonstrated purchase frequency. Estimated addressable base: 12 to 18M users globally. These are habitual trackers with explicit nutritional goals, not casual health app browsers.

Ad Targeting Gap

Instacart Ads targets search and browse behavior inside the app. Users are already in purchase mode. The platform misses the upstream intent signal that happens when someone plans what to eat: the moment most predictive of what they will buy.

MFP Monetization

MyFitnessPal holds 200M+ user profiles with granular dietary data, goal history, and macro patterns. Its current model does not monetize purchase intent. The data exists. The commerce layer does not.

CPG Brand Reach

CPG brands want to reach health-conscious, high-intent grocery buyers at the moment a need is identified, not after it. No ad platform currently offers this. Instacart can be first.

Customer Acquisition

MFP's 200M+ users represent an addressable market that has never seen an Instacart checkout flow. It is a low-CAC acquisition surface sitting unused.

02 · Solution

Four layers. One sequencing decision.

A formal API partnership between Instacart and MyFitnessPal, structured in four progressive layers. Layer 2 is the core monetization insight, but it cannot be trusted until Layer 1 establishes which sessions carry real purchase intent. Shipping Layer 2 without Layer 1 would fire prompts indiscriminately, destroying the relevance score that makes the ad surface premium before it exists. Layer 1 is not a feature. It is a prerequisite.

Force-ranked priority: Layer 2 is the core bet. Layer 1 protects its signal quality. Layer 3 monetizes it. Layer 4 compounds retention. If only one layer shipped, it would be Layer 2.

1

Weekly Grocery Intelligence

A lightweight prompt each week asks which days you are cooking versus eating out. Three taps. The integration builds one Instacart basket covering only home-cooking days, calibrated to macro goals. No prompts on eat-out days. No over-ordering. The basket is a suggestion you edit freely. It is not a meal plan.

Business Impact

Drives higher-quality weekly orders with lower abandonment. Creates a habitual weekly Instacart touchpoint anchored to an existing MFP routine.

2

Macro-Triggered Cart Builder

On home-cooking days, when a user logs a nutritional gap in MFP (a protein deficit, a low carb day, a micronutrient shortfall), the integration surfaces a Shop on Instacart prompt with pre-populated product suggestions matching the gap. One tap. On eat-out days, the integration stands down entirely.

Business Impact

Drives net-new Instacart sessions from a non-Instacart surface. Only fires when contextually relevant, protecting the relevance score that makes the ad surface premium.

3

Sponsored Product Placement

CPG brands bid to appear in the triggered cart based on the user's logged deficit and dietary profile. A user with a protein gap sees a sponsored Quest bar alongside organic suggestions. Ad placement is contextually earned, not interruption-based.

Business Impact

Creates a new sponsored placement surface with higher relevance scores than standard Instacart Ads. Commands premium CPMs and unlocks CPG budgets allocated to health and fitness verticals.

4

Persistent Nutrition-Aware Shopping

For connected accounts, Instacart's browse experience becomes macro-aware. Health Tags surface based on active goals. Weekly lists are pre-seeded with recurring nutrition needs. Substitution logic prioritizes nutritional equivalence.

Business Impact

Drives retention and basket size among health-conscious users. Creates a defensible moat: Instacart becomes the default grocery platform for anyone who tracks what they eat.

04 · Success Metrics

One north star. Clear kill criteria.

This spec is anchored to the Ads team. The north star is ROAS on the MFP-triggered ad surface versus the 2.1x Instacart Ads baseline. Everything else is diagnostic.

North Star
Sponsored Placement ROAS on MFP-Triggered Sessions
ROAS for CPG placements in triggered carts vs Instacart Ads baseline (2.1x). Target: 3.5x+ by end of Phase 2. If ROAS does not exceed baseline by 1.2x after 8 weeks of Phase 2, the premium ad surface thesis is not validated and Phase 3 should not proceed.
Secondary
Conversion Rate on MFP-Triggered Sessions
Percentage of users who tap a cart prompt and complete an order. Phase 1 go/no-go: 15%+, based on Instacart's ~12% browse-to-order baseline. Below baseline conversion means the intent signal is not strong enough to monetize.
Secondary
Net New Customer Acquisition via MFP
First-time Instacart customers whose first order originates from MFP. Secondary because acquisition is a growth team metric. Track as a lagging indicator of partnership distribution value.
Secondary
Basket Size for Nutrition-Goal Users
Average order value for MFP-connected users versus Instacart baseline. Target: 15%+ AOV lift. Validates that goal-driven shopping produces higher-value orders, the core retailer value prop.
Guardrail
Ad Relevance Score and Opt-Out Rate
User-reported relevance and integration opt-out rate. Kill threshold: opt-out rate above 8% in Phase 1. High opt-outs signal bad targeting or privacy discomfort. Both invalidate the premium surface thesis.
Guardrail
Data Privacy Complaint Rate
Volume of user complaints about nutrition data usage in ad targeting. Any spike above 0.5% of connected accounts triggers an immediate pause. Given Instacart's recent FTC settlement, another privacy incident is not a recoverable event.
05 · Risks and Mitigations

What could go wrong. And how we handle it.

Surfacing real risks early is part of the work. Good product thinking acknowledges constraints and proposes mitigations rather than building on optimistic assumptions.

RiskWhy It MattersMitigation
Partnership deal structure
MFP is owned by Francisco Partners, a PE firm whose interests are commercial, not strategic. A deal requires revenue sharing, data governance, and legal alignment across two independently operated platforms. Without a clear economic model, MFP has no incentive to prioritize this integration.
Proposed structure: Instacart offers MFP a 15 to 20% revenue share on sponsored placement revenue generated from MFP-sourced sessions, plus a fixed co-marketing budget. Phase 1 uses only aggregate goal data (no raw log access) to minimize data governance complexity. Co-marketing partnership first, deeper API second.
User privacy backlash
Health data is highly sensitive. Users may feel their nutrition goals are being used to target them commercially. Post-FTC settlement, Instacart's trust capital is already under pressure.
Explicit layered consent at account connection. Opt-in only for ad personalization, separate from data sharing for cart suggestions. Clear UI disclosures throughout. Pause immediately if complaint rate exceeds 0.5% of connected accounts.
MFP data quality
MFP's logged data is user-generated and inconsistent. Many users log meals after eating, not before. Intent inference requires sufficient data density to be actionable.
Target users with 30-plus day logging streaks for Phase 1. Build fallback logic to macro goal settings, which are more stable, when recent logs are sparse.
Low activation rate
The hypothesis depends on users acting on macro-triggered prompts. If the handoff creates too much friction, conversion will be low and the ad surface loses its premium positioning before CPG brands see meaningful ROAS data.
Deep-link directly into a pre-built Instacart cart with items already added. Minimize taps between prompt and checkout. A/B test trigger timing. Phase 1 kill threshold: if conversion rate is below 15% after 8 weeks, pause and diagnose before Phase 2.
06 · Phased Rollout

How to ship this without betting everything on it.

Stage the build so each phase validates the core hypothesis before committing to the next layer of complexity. Kill criteria are defined. No phase is contingent on another to deliver standalone value.

Phase 1 · Q3 2026 · 8 weeks

Pilot: Macro-to-Cart, No Ads

Build the MFP to Instacart cart handoff using macro goal data only. Opt-in pilot with 50K MFP power users. Measure conversion rate and cart completion. No sponsored placements yet. Validate the demand signal before monetizing it.

Phase 2 · Q4 2026 · 12 weeks

Monetize: Sponsored Placements

If Phase 1 conversion exceeds the 15% threshold, introduce sponsored product placements in the triggered cart suggestions. Onboard 5 to 10 health-focused CPG brands for the beta. Measure ROAS delta versus standard Instacart Ads. Target: 3.5x.

Phase 3 · Q1 2027 · Ongoing

Deepen: Persistent Nutrition-Aware Instacart

For connected accounts, bring macro-awareness into the full Instacart experience across Health Tags, smart substitutions, and goal-seeded weekly lists. Scale CPG brand partnerships. Explore equivalent integrations with Cronometer, MacroFactor, and Whoop's food logging feature.

07 · Why I'm Proposing This

I found this gap because I fell into it.

I track macros on MyFitnessPal. I order groceries on Instacart. One evening I logged dinner, saw I was 42g short on protein, and spent the next 20 minutes on Google cross-referencing protein content with what was available for delivery. I ended up ordering something that did not quite match what I needed.

Both apps had everything they needed to solve that for me. Neither one did. The gap is not a missing feature. It is a missing connection. That is what this spec is.

The best products get built by people who notice the gap and cannot stop thinking about it. I have not stopped thinking about this one.
